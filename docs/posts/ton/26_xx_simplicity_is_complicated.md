# Simplicity is Complicated

In case it is not apparent yet, I will admit that I use this blog primarily as a motivation to research a concept that is unclear to me in the hope to satisfy some weird curiosity.
And this time, the concept is _simplicity_.

It fascinates me, because it is really hard to grasp.
One the one hand I admire simple things.
Especially, when an otherwise complicated topic becomes apparent and clear, this is something that fills me with genuine joy.
On the other hand, things that are too simple can feel wrong.
It makes you wonder whether you are not given the whole truth.
This way, doubting the simplicity of your own understanding can destroy your whole worldview.

I remember a conversation with a colleague, explaining them what I appreciate in research papers.
The ideal engineering-science paper for me is based on one simple idea.
This way it is easy to explain on the limited pages, and of course it will only be a good engineering paper if it manages to demonstrate that it is very effective at solving the addressed problem.
But my co-worker answered that I must be careful with this, because reviews can be very challenging.
This left me confused, because it didn't occur to me that something being simple could be seen as bad in terms of quality.

This illustrates the main confusion:
Why is simplicity a property that can contradict excellence?
But there are more questions:
What is it about simplicity that is so hard to grasp?
And, how can something become so simple, it becomes wrong?

## What is simplicity

In the philosophical literature simplicity is often discussed in the context of theories.
A theory being a theoretical construct that is compared to perceptions of the practical subject at hand.
